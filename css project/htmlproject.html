El Salvador

Country Background

In 1821, El Salvador was able to achieve independence from Spain. El Salvador was achieved it from the Central American Federation in the year of 1839. One of the world's highest homicide rates and universal criminal gangs. The flag has three equal horizontal bands of cobalt blue, white and cobalt blue with the national coat of arms that is centered in the white band. The emblem reads, "REPUBLICA DE EL SALVADOR EN LA AMERICA CENTRAL".

People and Society

El Salvador is the smallest and most densely populated country in C.A. The country is well into its dempographic transition, experiencing slower population growth and decrease in its number of youths. The usage of family planning has lowered El Salvador's fertility rate. Fertility differences are a factor between the rich and the poor and urban and rural women which are diminishing.

Government

The current Chief of State is President Nayib Armando Bukele Ortez. He has been in office since June 1, 2019. The vice president is Felix Augusto Antonio Ulloa Garay. The president is both chief of state and head of government.

Demographic

The increased use of family planning has lowered El Salvador's fertility rate, from nearly 6 children per woman in the 1970s to replacement level. A 2008 national family planning survey demonstrated that female sterilization remains the most common method in El Salvador. Its sterilization rate is within the highest in Latin America. The use of injectable strategies is growing. Fertility differences between the rich and the poor and the rural women are narrowing. Salvadorans fled during the civil war to the United States but also to Canada and other cities. Emigration to the United States has increased again in the 1990s and 2000s as a result of natural disasters and family reunification. At least 20% of El Salvador's population has been living abroad.
